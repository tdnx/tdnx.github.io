My first introduction to the world of crime, mystery and thriller. Also, my first love! The inception of my love for Prof. Robert Langdon is credited to this book. 

I have always been an admirer of Leonardo DaVinci. I was simply mesmerized by the fact that how can one human being explore so much in his lifetime. He knew how to explore! How to think! How to innovate! A mind like him is really precious. In today's world, we try to create divisions between subjects. Strictly claim that a topic belongs to chemistry or physics or what-not. But I had started to think that there is no division between arts and science. Atleast Walter Issacson would agree with me, no?

A book that explored (almost at a conspiracy theory level) Leonardo's life's work was something that inspires me. And someone tried to take those hints and create a mystery thriller? I was sold! Fun fact, I also visited the Roslin chapel that is mentioned on the book and the movie (wouldn't make much sense if I hadn't, given that I work in ROSLIN Institute). And ofcourse, there, I listened to *Chevaliers de Sangreal* by Hans Zimmer (Ofcouse, I had to!).

You know what's my favourite line in the book? When Silas says: "*Pain is good!*"
